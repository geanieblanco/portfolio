Social Media Marketing

Every day over 750 million users log into Facebook, making it the most trafficked social networking website in the history of the Internet. Not having an effective social media plan means leaving out millions of potential customers, relationships, and marketing opportunities. My social media approach consists of three important concepts: Voice, Content, and Execution.

Voice: Picking out a voice for your business is just as important as your physical appearance. Finding the essence of your business, whether it's mature and serious, young and funny, or something entirely its own, helps to ensure that your brand is communicable to your targeted audience.

Content: Great content leads to great reactions. Keeping your business content clean, non-confrontational, and friendly will encourage readers to maintain connection to your business.

Execution: Knowing when to, or when not to post content makes sure that you're not overwhelming your audience, or not keeping them engaged enough. Having a publication schedule keeps the reader ready to find out what new and exciting updates your brand has.

Event Marketing

Very rarely do people leave a website and think "That was a great experience"; those types of reactions are reserved for in-person interactions with people and product. Hosting a marketing event can do wonders for your business: it gives you the chance to interact with your consumers creating a feeling or corporate trust between business and customer, it also gives the customer a chance to sample your work before committing. The worst type of bad experience is the one that you had to pay for.

Creative Strategy: Marketing events generally take place as a vendor in a larger event, or as a stand alone pop-up event. During your event samples of your product or service are shown to the public by either your employees or hired brand representatives. All aspects of the event from decor, to time of day are set to make sure your brand is shown in its natural form.

Execution: Focusing on the business's goal audience is vital to creating a good marketing event. Having a balloon-animal making clown at an event for a liquor store will not only leave your visitors confused, but can also convey an irresponsible message. Keying into the needs and demographics of your audience makes sure that you represent your business in the best possible way.
